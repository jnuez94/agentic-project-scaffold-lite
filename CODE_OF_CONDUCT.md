# Code Of Conduct

This project exists to help people coordinate serious multi-agent work with clarity, accountability, and respect.

## Expected Behavior

- Be direct without being dismissive.
- Critique artifacts, decisions, and assumptions, not people.
- State uncertainty clearly.
- Keep feedback specific and actionable.
- Respect role boundaries and decision rights.
- Protect private, sensitive, regulated, and proprietary information.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, or personal attacks.
- Publishing private information without permission.
- Introducing sensitive data into public examples or coordination records.
- Misrepresenting review, approval, security, release, or production readiness.
- Using ambiguity to bypass decision rights.

## Enforcement

For a public project, maintainers should define:

- who receives conduct reports
- how reports are reviewed
- what actions maintainers may take
- how conflicts of interest are handled

Until then, project maintainers are responsible for keeping collaboration healthy and safe.
